Retired's Lotto Analysis

June 3, 2025

Happy to sip with you a coffee on this .... but leave if it does not resonate

I have been writing about important messages so far, uh? Okay, now I found something possibly interesting but much lighter: German Lotto !!

Looking at Ger,am Lotto draws I have been always hit by the almost constant presence of contiguous numbers. Is this a real pattern? SO I decided to go deeper in that: here the draws from 2018

After analyzing 774 lottery draws from 2018 onwards, I discovered significantly more patterns than expected for truly random draws. While each individual draw remains unpredictable, the historical sequence reveals fascinating statistical behaviors that challenge the assumption of pure randomness.

üîçKey Pattern Discoveries

1. Number Persistence - The Most Striking Finding

55% of draws share at least one number with the previous draw!

This is far higher than the ~37% expected for pure randomness.

  • No shared numbers: 349 draws (45.1%)
  • 1 shared number: 324 draws (41.9%)
  • 2 shared numbers: 83 draws (10.7%)
  • 3 shared numbers: 17 draws (2.2%)

Example: January 20, 2018 [3, 11, 15, 18, 33, 45] followed by January 24, 2018 [3, 5, 18, 31, 37, 45] sharing three numbers: [3, 18, 45]

2. Sequential Number Patterns

  • 3-number sequences: 31 draws (4.0%) contain three consecutive numbers
  • 4-number sequences: 1 draw (0.1%) contained four consecutive numbers
  • Companion pairs (2 consecutive): 367 draws (47.4%)

Examples of 3-number sequences:

  • July 25, 2018: [39-40-41]
  • August 4, 2018: [29-30-31]
  • February 16, 2019: [9-10-11]

3. Mathematical Relationships

51.3% of draws contain at least one a + b = c relationship!

This suggests the lottery machine may have subtle biases toward mathematically related numbers.

Examples:

  • January 3, 2018: 10 + 35 = 45 in [10, 15, 31, 34, 35, 45]
  • January 6, 2018: 5 + 32 = 37 in [5, 14, 32, 37, 46, 47]

4. Hot and Cold Streaks

  • Longest cold streak: Number 21 went missing for 68 consecutive draws
  • Most frequent number: Number 1 appeared 114 times (19.2 above expected)
  • Least frequent number: Number 39 appeared only 71 times (23.8 below expected)

5. Sum Distribution Clustering

  • Average sum: 147.5 (very close to theoretical 150)
  • Range: 58 to 264 (span of 206)
  • 44.1% of draws cluster within ¬±20 of the average

Extreme examples:

  • Lowest: April 3, 2019 [2, 5, 7, 9, 13, 22] = 58
  • Highest: March 2, 2019 [38, 41, 42, 46, 48, 49] = 264

6. Additional Mathematical Patterns

  • Arithmetic progressions: 182 draws (23.5%) contain 3+ numbers in arithmetic sequence
  • Fibonacci numbers: 40 draws (5.2%) contain 3+ Fibonacci numbers
  • Perfect squares: 27 draws (3.5%) contain 3+ perfect squares
  • Prime numbers: Variable distribution from 0-6 primes per draw

7. Decade Distribution

Numbers are not evenly distributed across ranges:

  • 1-10: Slightly underrepresented
  • 11-20: Close to expected
  • 21-30: Close to expected
  • 31-40: Close to expected
  • 41-49: Slightly overrepresented

By definition, Lotto MUST be under normal distribution constraints. But the drawing machine might have flows and the dataset is limited, so every insights will have to be correctly understood. Nevertheless, one critical question remains unanswered: one should bet on overrepresented or underrepresented dataset? Roulette still teaches here? ..... ahah!!!
